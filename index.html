Research expertise and contributions

I am an interdisciplinary scientist dedicated to investigating the intertwined crises of human health, climate change and environmental degradation. My research employs a transdisciplinary lens and mixed methods to bridge health and sustainability perspectives. I examine how physical, mental and social human health are impacted by environmental changes, including climate change and biodiversity loss. For example, I have investigated how nature-based solutions impact human health through mitigating climate change effects Gillerot et al., Global Change Biology. I have also explored drivers of pro-environmental behaviours, identifying how societal values and social norms shape these behaviours (Sockhill et al., People and Nature; Oh et al., People and Nature).
